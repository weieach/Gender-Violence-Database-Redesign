by Reilly Brady

Introducing the Gender Violence Database: Theoretical, Practical, and Pedagogical Challenges of Mapping a Field

In addition to offering a general overview of the Gender Violence Database (GVD for short) as a tool, this paper first traces how our project team has confronted the research-practice divide in the gender violence field that gave rise to our project in the first place– and then the paper offers our project as a model for feminist DH pedagogy.

Critical Reflection

Religion and the Gender Violence Database: Reflections, Difficulties, and Remaining Gaps

While working on the Gender Violence Database throughout 2023, I focused on the ways in which religion and religious identity can shape experiences of gender-based violence as well as paths of healing for survivors. During my previous work with the Gender Violence Database, I noticed an emerging awareness of the specific religious contexts that can shape experiences of gender violence; in the social work journals I had previously focused on, this translated to a need for culturally-sensitive approaches in therapy and other social work fields. However, there seemed to be a general lack of scholarship within gender violence-focused journals addressing religious contexts of gender-based violence both in terms of culturally-specific approaches and concerning broader implications about religious institutions serving as forces of harm as well as as spaces for healing for survivors.
