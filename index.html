Gender Violence Database

An ongoing project to collect interdisciplinary academic research related to gender-based violence.

Scroll down to learn more

What counts as gender-based violence in the GVD?

Our Scope

Our scope includes forms of harm committed on the basis of gender, that are gendered in action, or that reinforce forms of gender oppression already in place. We include research focused on forms of harm such as intimate partner violence, sexual violence, harassment, stalking, trafficking as they affect adults or children in any place in the world. We also include research that addresses the consequences of gender-based violence and that proposes intervention and/or prevention strategies.

Tagging Workflow

We assign tags to articles within a shared Zotero library that feeds into the database. The team operates on feminist consensus building through weekly meetings to address issues around research concepts and identify gaps within the collection. Team members include faculty, support from the Humanities Digital Workshop, librarians, graduate students, and undergraduate students from various fields. More than forty undergraduate students have contributed to the project since its conception in 2010, with active periods ranging from a single term to multiple years.
