About Us

The Gender Violence Database addresses a longstanding divide between researchers and practitioners in the field of gender-based violence. It is designed to be a searchable tool for researchers, practitioners, and the general public to access current and past research.

For practitioners, there has been an increasing emphasis on using evidence-based practices—that is, approaches and interventions that have been proven to be effective through rigorous research. One of the challenges of finding and implementing such practices is that many practitioners do not have easy access to the research that demonstrates effectiveness or that might inform their practice choices in other ways. Accessing current research often involves a considerable investment of time and money on the part of practitioners and organizations who may most need it.

Because the field of gender-based violence is fairly young and multidisciplinary, researchers may also face challenges in identifying and accessing relevant research, since there are not consistent terms or categories that researchers employ across the many disciplines that the field comprises.
